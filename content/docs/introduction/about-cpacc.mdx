## About the CPACC Certification

The **Certified Professional in Accessibility Core Competencies (CPACC)** is a foundational certification offered by the **International Association of Accessibility Professionals (IAAP)**. It validates broad, cross-disciplinary knowledge in accessibility, universal design, disability inclusion, and accessibility-related laws and standards.

## Certification Process

To earn the CPACC credential, candidates must pass an exam that evaluates knowledge in three key domains:

1. **Disabilities, Challenges, and Assistive Technologies** _(40% of the exam)_

   - Theoretical models of disability
   - Categories of disabilities and associated barriers
   - Assistive technologies and adaptive strategies
   - Disability demographics and statistics
   - Disability etiquette

2. **Accessibility and Universal Design** _(40% of the exam)_

   - Individual accommodations vs. inclusive design
   - Benefits of accessibility
   - ICT accessibility (e.g., WCAG 2.1)
   - Physical world accessibility (Universal Design principles)
   - Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
   - Accessibility, usability, and user experience (UX)

3. **Standards, Laws, and Management Strategies** _(20% of the exam)_
   - International conventions and treaties on disability rights
   - Categories of disability laws and regulations
   - Applying accessibility standards to ICT
   - Organizational governance and management

Passing the CPACC exam demonstrates foundational expertise in accessibility and is suitable for professionals in both technical and non-technical roles.

## Related Certifications

- **Web Accessibility Specialist (WAS):** A more technical certification for professionals working in web accessibility.
- **Certified Professional in Web Accessibility (CPWA):** A higher-level credential awarded to individuals who pass both the CPACC and WAS exams.
